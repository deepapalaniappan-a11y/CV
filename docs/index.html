About

My work is on disability inclusion, accessibility and policy, alongside people with diverse disabilities across grassroots, government, university and international contexts, with a field grounding in Disability Inclusive Development. I am currently an independent consultant on accessibility, training and policy. From December 2022 to March 2026 I was with the Office of Disability Inclusion at Azim Premji University, covering reasonable accommodations, assistive technology and accessible-document support.

Two APW engagements with the WHO Disability Unit in Geneva contributed to the Global Report on Health Equity for Persons with Disabilities (2022). As Short Term Consultant with the World Bank, I supported the TN RIGHTS project with the Government of Tamil Nadu. As State Disability Consultant with JEEVIKA, the Bihar Rural Livelihoods Promotion Society, I developed the State Disability Inclusion Guideline, in implementation since 2020. I held an honorary Distinguished Fellowship at the Kalam Institute of Health Technology, a WHO Collaborating Centre, from 2023 to 2025.

Alongside this I run EquitableDocs, an accessible-documents initiative for students with print disabilities in the Global South, and the Disability Rights Repository, a small community archive for grassroots disability practitioners.

Initiatives

Disability Rights Repository

A community archive that gives visibility to grassroots disability practitioners whose work rarely reaches mainstream platforms. The repository collects practice notes, training materials, field documentation and lived-experience accounts that otherwise live only on local drives, and offers a publishing space for OPD leaders and patient advocates to grow their own documentation capacity.

EquitableDocs

An accessible-document initiative that checks academic and institutional PDFs against accessibility standards, repairs what software alone cannot, sends the readable file back, and trains the originating team so future documents start out accessible. Built for Global South universities, government departments, libraries and NGO partners. Free for students, faculty, library staff and government; cost-only for institutions, with no margin added.
