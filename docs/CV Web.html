Deepa Palaniappan

Disability inclusion / Accessibility / Policy and research

Working on disability inclusion and accessibility for over a decade, with hands-on experience alongside people with diverse disabilities across grassroots, government, university and international settings. Day-to-day focus is on Accessibility, Assistive Technology, Training and Policy work. Coordinating an Accessible Document Initiative, EquitableDocs, a non-commercial pipeline for accessible academic content in the Global South, and the Disability Rights Repository, a community archive that gives visibility to grassroots disability practitioners and supports documentation by OPD leaders and patient advocates. Earlier work includes two consultancies with the WHO Disability Unit in Geneva, CBR work with the World Bank for the Government of Tamil Nadu, and a period in Bihar centred on hands-on grassroots work with Self Help Groups for Persons with Disabilities, which led to the drafting of the State Disability Inclusion Guideline in use across the Bihar Rural Livelihoods Mission since 2020. Currently Advisor for Inclusion, Systems and Partnerships at Snehadhara Foundation, Bengaluru.

05Initiatives

Two Initiatives.

A community archive and an accessible-documents pipeline.
disability-rights-repository.org

Disability Rights Repository

A community archive that gives visibility to grassroots disability practitioners whose work rarely reaches mainstream platforms. The repository collects practice notes, training materials, field documentation and lived-experience accounts that often otherwise live only on local drives, and offers a publishing space for OPD leaders and patient advocates to grow their own documentation capacity.

Visit site
equitabledocs.org

EquitableDocs

An Accessible Document Initiative that checks academic and institutional PDFs against accessibility standards, repairs what software alone cannot, sends the readable file back, and trains the originating team so future documents start out accessible. Built for Global South universities, government departments, libraries and NGO partners. Free for students, faculty, library staff and government, and cost-only for institutions, with no margin added.

Visit site
Accessible Documents Community of Practice

A peer group for document accessibility practitioners.

Practitioners working on document accessibility across the Global South meet periodically to share remediation approaches, tooling and field experience. The interest form takes about three minutes.

Interest form, Document Accessibility CoP
